The syntax of CTL is described below.

#e# e a regular predicate (regular valuations) described as following table.
identifier Atomic proposition is an alphanumeric string with underscore _, colon : and dollar mark $ except for keywords. Case is significant.
!f The negation of the CTL formula f.
f || g The disjunction of the CTL formulas f and g.
f && g The conjunction of the CTL formulas f and g.
f -> g The CTL formula f implies the CTL formula g.
f <-> g f -> g and g ->f.
EX f For some computation path, next time f.
AX f For all computation paths, next time f.
EF f For some computation path, eventually f.
AF f For all computation paths, eventually f.
EG f For some computation path, always f.
AG f For all computation paths, always f.
E[f U g] For some computation path, always f until g.
A[f U g] For all computation paths, always f until g.
E[f W g] It equals to !A[!f U !g].
A[f W g] It equals to !E[!f U !g].
(f) The CTL formula f.

The syntax of Regular Predicates (Regular valuations) is given in regular expression described below.

identifier Matching the word identifier in any alphanumeric string with underscore _, colon : and dollar mark $ begin with alphanumeric, except for keywords. Case is significant.
. Matching any word.
| The concatenation operator.
+ The choice operator.
* Kleene closure.
(e) The regular expression e.

The syntax of Alternating Buechi Pushdown Systems.

Alternating Buechi Pushdown System is given in the following form

(p0 <r0>) The initial configuration with control location p0 and the stack content r0. PuMoC outputs a finite automaton if the initial configuraiton is not given.

ACCEPTING p18,p19 The accepting control locations.

p0 <r0> --> p1 < > Pop transition
p4 <r2> --> p2 <r2> Rewrite transition
p4 <r3> --> p4 <r2 r3> Push transition
p1 <r1> --> p18 <r3 r1> & p19 <r0 r3> Alternating transition

The syntax of Pushdown Systems.

PuMoC can process the syntax of pushdown systems given in Moped and PDSolver.

The syntax of Boolean Programs.

PuMoC can process the syntax of boolean programs given in SLAM and Satabs except the concurrent instructions .
